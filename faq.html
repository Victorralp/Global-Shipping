What shipping methods do you offer?

We offer a variety of shipping methods to meet different needs and budgets:

  • Ocean Freight: FCL (Full Container Load) and LCL (Less than Container Load) options for cost-effective international shipping.
  • Air Freight: Express and standard air shipping for time-sensitive deliveries worldwide.
  • Ground Transportation: Truck and rail options for domestic and cross-border shipping.
  • Specialized Services: Temperature-controlled shipping, hazardous materials handling, and oversized cargo transportation.

Our logistics experts can help you determine the best method based on your timeline, budget, and cargo specifications.

How long will my shipment take to arrive?

Delivery times vary depending on the shipping method, origin, destination, and any customs clearance requirements. Here are general timeframes:

  • Domestic Ground Shipping: 1-5 business days
  • International Air Freight: 3-10 business days
  • International Ocean Freight: 15-45 business days
  • Express Shipping: 1-3 business days (depending on destination)

For a more accurate estimate, please use our shipping calculator or contact customer service with your specific details.

What items are prohibited from shipping?

While the exact list varies by country and shipping method, generally prohibited items include:

  • Illegal substances and narcotics
  • Weapons, ammunition, and explosives
  • Flammable materials (unless shipped as declared hazardous goods)
  • Perishable goods (without proper refrigeration)
  • Live animals (except through specialized services)
  • Human remains
  • Counterfeit goods
  • Currency and high-value items without proper insurance

Please consult our detailed prohibited items list or contact customer service if you're unsure about a specific item.

What if my tracking information hasn't updated?

Tracking information may sometimes experience delays in updating for several reasons:

  • Packages in transit between scanning facilities
  • System delays in information transfer
  • Customs inspection periods
  • Weather or logistical delays

If your tracking hasn't updated for more than 48 hours, please contact our customer service team for assistance. We can provide more detailed information about your shipment's status.

How do customs and duties work for international shipments?

International shipments are subject to customs clearance, which may include duties and taxes:

  • Customs duties are taxes imposed by a country on imported goods.
  • Rates vary based on the product type, country of origin, and destination country's regulations.
  • Most countries have de minimis thresholds below which no duties are charged.
  • The recipient is typically responsible for paying duties and taxes unless you choose our DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) option.

Our customs brokerage service can help navigate complex customs requirements, prepare documentation, and in some cases, pre-pay duties to streamline the delivery process.

What documents are required for international shipping?

For international shipments, you'll typically need the following documents:

  • Commercial Invoice: Details of the goods, their value, and the transaction
  • Packing List: Itemized list of the package contents
  • Certificate of Origin: Document certifying where the goods were manufactured
  • Customs Declaration Form: Official form declaring the nature of the shipment
  • Special Permits/Licenses: For restricted items (if applicable)

Additional documents may be required depending on the product type and destination country. Our team can provide guidance on specific requirements for your shipment.

How are shipping costs calculated?

Shipping costs are calculated based on several factors:

  • Dimensional Weight: The package's volume (length × width × height)
  • Actual Weight: The physical weight of the package
  • Distance: How far the shipment is traveling
  • Service Level: Express or standard delivery options
  • Special Handling: Requirements like temperature control or hazardous materials
  • Fuel Surcharges: Additional fees based on current fuel prices

We charge based on either the dimensional weight or actual weight, whichever is greater. For an accurate quote, please use our online calculator or contact our sales team.

Do you offer shipping insurance?

Yes, we offer shipping insurance to protect your goods against loss or damage during transit:

  • Basic coverage is included for shipments up to $100 in value.
  • Additional insurance can be purchased for higher-value shipments.
  • Coverage rates are typically 1-3% of the declared value, depending on the goods and route.
  • Claims must be filed within 30 days of the scheduled delivery date.

We strongly recommend insurance for all high-value or critical shipments. Our team can help you determine the right level of coverage for your needs.
